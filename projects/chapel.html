Souvenirs - MIT Chapel

MIT 4.022 class, Feb 2023 - May 2023

Class Project

Design/Architecture

In Mit 4.022 -Introduction to Design Techniques and Technologies- we are asked to develop a series of souvenirs that questions the resolution and legibility through disciplined acts of miniaturization. With my partner, we selected the MIT Chaple to start a series of works through different media.

Skills

  • Rhinoceros 3D
  • Model Casting
  • Visual Creation
  • Paper Mache/Spraypaint

Collaborator: Frederick Hoffman

Project Description

This assignment was based on the MIT Chapel, a cylindircal building created with brick. A detail about the chapel that inspired me and my partner was the hand-selected bricks of varying shapes and sizes. Some of the bricks protrude more than others, creating an interesting shadow. We were interested in seeing how much of the chapel could be understood with as little information as possible. The approach we took was to vary the number of bricks shown, the shadows, and the pattern used to represent bricks. These overall direct our investigation into the 2D and 3D miniatures.

Image: MIT Chapel

Two-Dimensional Drawings and Reliefs

In this investigation, we wanted to highlight the “special bricks,” which consisted of many sizes and shapes that protruded from the chapel. We examined the bricks on the chapel and created a set of notable bricks that we would use throughout our mold. Experimenting with spraypaint and the laser cutters was important as well, as we had to find and use the limits of legibility with new materials.

Three-Dimensional Miniatures

The final rendition of the MIT Chapel posed the most difficulties in terms of material. Working with Rockite for the first time was a great challenge and inspired many of our choices for this project. Thinking about our previous renditions and their cartoonish nature, we created a toy with bricks that can be removed, swapped, and put back into the chapel's form. This also allowed us to have detailed bricks without the rocking cracking and being illegible. We 3D printed colorful bricks of different designs, ranging from basic long bricks to colorful bricks with dramatic sizes and shapes. These final four souvenirs are meant to combine the sentiment behind all of our previous works with the chapel, looking at low resolution, special bricks, and having a great emphasis on the overall form of the chapel. These 4 took experimentation with much different fabrication processes, and took multiple ideas that developed through the use of multiple different mediums.
