Spinning Hologram

MIT Dorm Project, Jan 2022 - May 2022

Personal Project

Control/Electronics/Design

In Spring 2022, I proposed a 3D display device to enable group discussions around a 3D model. MIT then funded this proposal, and it turned into my dorm project. I designed a high-rpm spinning LED matrix with a fast, low-level update code. This allows a 3D video to be displayed in the middle of the air.

Project Goal

A volumetric hologram is designed for this project. A high-rpm spinning LED matrix with a fast, low-level update code is used to achieve this goal. I allow the LED matrix to display in both directions so there will be no barrier to seeing it from both sides. This will allow people to upload their CAD into the hologram, and it'll display the file for people to view it from all angles.

Results

I use Matlab to generate and simulate the color of the individual LEDs at certian timing and postions. I then combine them into files that are preloaded into the micro controller. After several steps of pre-calculation, the system can reach the following specs:

Design Decisions

On the structural side, rotary shafts with self-aligning bearings are chosen. The frame is 3dp to minimize mass imbalance. I iterated through different motors and eventually decided on a brushless motor with a hall effect encoder to give enough power. I first used PID control for the control and then switched to pure open loop control to get more accurate angle values. The program is coded on esp32 with a high clock rate. Individual addressable with parallel output is used to maximize the update rate
