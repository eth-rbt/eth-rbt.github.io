Amplify
- Hand Motion Amplification Device

Make MIT 2023, Feb 2023

Hackathon Project

Control/ProductDesign/Eelctronics

Carpal tunnel syndrome affects more than 3 million people, causing tingling and numbness of the hand and arm and impairing daily activity. One of our teammates experiences Carpal Tunnel and can not perform high torque load motions. In 24 hours, we built a grasping system that senses motion from your hand through a sleeve interface. The claw can perform grabbing, bending, and twisting/turning movements. All the load is on the forearm, so the wrist is free from being loaded.

Award: Best Use of Sensors

Collaborators:

  • Aileen Liao
  • Andrew Woo
  • Garrett Blosen
  • Oliver Trevor

Result

We designed our gripper gear and motor mechanisms compactly. We also focused on user interfaces with various intuitive sensing modalities, including force and position sensing. The end product is a 3-DoF claw that allows amplification of twisting, grabbing, and turning force. It follows the user's gesture by the sensors we embedded in the glove and computed through a PID control scheme.

Chord-inate
-Iot Embedded Self Playing Guitar

Make MIT 2022, Feb 2022

Hackathon Project

Control/ProductDesign/Electronics

Covid blocked the opportunity for live music. It might be helpful to design a device that will enable live music to play through the help of IOT. In 24 hours, we developed a solenoid-actuated system that can be strapped onto any guitar, with a website to which any user can connect and access the guitar. This device also allows users to play music with friends virtually.

Award: First Place
