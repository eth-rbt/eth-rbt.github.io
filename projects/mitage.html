Supportive Indoor Footwear Design
for the Aging Population

MIT Age Lab, Jan 2022- May 2022

Student Research Project

ProductDesign/Ergonomics

How might we rethink footwear for older people? In this project, I collaborated with the MIT Age Lab and Designer Sheng-Hung Lee to discover the possibility of redesigning indoor footwear. Sketches and Interviews are made to examine diverse ways to facilitate older people through ergonomic design.

Problem Statement/ Identifying Needs

Adult-acquired flatfoot is a debilitating condition that affects up to 5 million people in the US. When people are over 40 years old, they have a chance of 50% of getting it. This makes standing and walking around more tiresome and further impacts the senior's life quality. Designs like these aren't available for indoor shoes. The older population spends more than 80% of their time indoors. Research shows that supportive shoe designs like these allow them to stand for 2 hours more daily.

Design Requirements

  • Adaptability: The indoor shoe needs to be adaptable to different degrees of flat-foot syndrome. It should also accommodate the difference in degrees of feet deformation.
  • Indications: The footwear should be able to exhibit colors or signals when the user is pressuring the sole in unwanted ways. This indication can either be electronic or based on material.
  • Comfort: The footwear and building components must be structurally sound and comfortable. Everyday indoor footwear has complex parts that will leave marks on feet. This footwear must be appealing to use.

Results

I hosted brainstorming sessions and collected reviews and inputs from older people. I designed multiple sketches from this information that boil down to a customizable, tunable supportive indoor footwear that can sense pressure in the inner feet arch. The shoe itself has a removable piece that allows people to try different forms of arch support. The arch support has holes that enable users to increment the height by other components. Under this region, specific sensors will be placed. This sensor can either be electrical or have simple stamp-like mechanisms. It can show the pressure distribution, and the user can adjust the supporting pieces accordingly.
